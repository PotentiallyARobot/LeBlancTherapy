About Katherine

Therapy That Honors Your Whole Self

I'm Katherine LeBlanc, a Licensed Independent Clinical Social Worker with over 10 years of experience in the field. I hold a Masters in Social Work and have dedicated my career to supporting individuals through life's most challenging moments.

I support young adults and adults navigating anxiety, ADHD, depression, grief, and relational trauma. My work blends evidence-based care with a humanistic, whole-person approach. Therapy with me is collaborative, practical, and deeply reflective—focused on helping you build emotional clarity, secure relationships, and sustainable change.

I take a "person in the environment" approach, thinking about your interactions and relationships within the micro-to-macro systems that surround you. Much of the deeper work done by my clients surrounds themes of perfectionism, childhood attachment wounds, and healing from relational trauma.

Services

How I Can Support You

I offer a range of counseling services tailored to meet your unique needs. Every therapeutic journey is different, and I'm here to meet you where you are.

Individual Therapy

One-on-one sessions focused on your unique challenges, goals, and growth. Together, we'll explore what's beneath the surface and build strategies for lasting change through collaborative, reflective work.

Couples Therapy

Navigate relationship challenges, improve communication, and strengthen your bond. I have expertise in helping both men and women who struggle with building intimacy in their relationships.

Telehealth Sessions

Access quality mental health care from the comfort of your home. Secure, HIPAA-compliant video sessions available for clients in Washington DC, Massachusetts, and California.

Family Therapy

Work through family dynamics, improve communication patterns, and build stronger connections. I help families navigate change, conflict, and transition together.

Parent Consultation

Standalone or short-term consultative sessions for parents seeking guidance around a child's emotional, behavioral, or developmental needs — without immediately enrolling the child in therapy.

Life Transition Support

Additional therapeutic support during periods of significant change such as divorce, grief, postpartum adjustment, caregiving, relocation, or major life decisions.

Ways I Support Clients Beyond Sessions

Extended Care & Support

Care that's personalized — not protocol-driven. Support that extends beyond the therapy hour when you need it.

All care is individualized. These options are designed to support continuity and responsiveness — not to standardize therapy.

Between-Session Support

When questions arise between appointments

Brief, secure messaging support between sessions for moments when questions arise, emotions feel overwhelming, or clarity is needed. Many challenges occur outside the therapy hour — this option allows care to continue in a supportive, contained way.

Boundaries are maintained to ensure care remains ethical and sustainable.

Care Continuity Notes

Personalized session reflections

Brief written reflections after sessions highlighting key themes, tools, and points of focus — designed to help you integrate therapy into daily life. Especially supportive for clients who want to remember, reflect, and apply insights between sessions.

Parent Consultation & Guidance

Support for families

Consultative sessions for parents seeking guidance around a child's emotional, behavioral, or developmental needs. This collaborative approach provides practical guidance and support without immediately enrolling the child in therapy.

This is not therapy for the child — it's support and guidance for you as a parent.

Care Coordination

Available when clinically appropriate

When helpful, I collaborate with schools, prescribers, pediatricians, or other providers to support continuity of care. This ensures everyone involved in your wellbeing is working together effectively.

Available when helpful and clinically appropriate.

My Approach

Collaborative, Practical & Deeply Reflective

I take a humanistic approach to support clients, focusing on your existing strengths, challenging limiting self-narratives, and building healthy patterns of behavior. I take a conversational tone in therapy, ensuring I discuss clinical topics in a way that is meaningful and easily applied to your daily life.

My approach to goal setting is all about collaborating to identify your focus, needs, core values, and what it means to be you. You are the expert in you — developing trust is always the first step to achieving any goals you bring to therapy.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you take insurance?

I work with a limited number of clients using insurance through Headway. Insurance availability depends on current caseload, and openings may not always be available.

What if I want to use insurance but no slots are available?

If insurance-covered openings are unavailable, you may choose to book a private-pay session or check back later for availability updates through Headway.

Can I combine insurance and private pay?

Private-pay and insurance-based services cannot be combined within the same session.

How do I book a session?

All sessions are scheduled through Headway, which provides secure booking and handles insurance verification when applicable. Click "Book Now (Headway)" to get started.

What is telehealth therapy like?

Telehealth sessions take place over secure video. Many clients find them just as personal and connected as in-person care, with the added convenience of being able to attend from home.

What states are you licensed in?

I am licensed to provide therapy in Washington DC, Massachusetts, and California. You must be physically located in one of these states during our sessions.
